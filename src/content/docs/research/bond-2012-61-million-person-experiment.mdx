> Bond, Robert M., Christopher J. Fariss, Jason J. Jones, Adam D. I. Kramer, Cameron Marlow, Jaime E. Settle, and James H. Fowler. 2012. "A 61-Million-Person Experiment in Social Influence and Political Mobilization." Nature 489(7415): 295–298.

**Source:** [View publication ↗](https://doi.org/10.1038/nature11421) · Published 2012

A randomized experiment delivered get-out-the-vote messages to 61 million Facebook users on Election Day 2010, comparing an informational message, a "social" message showing friends who had voted, and a control. The social message increased turnout directly (~60,000 votes) and, via social contagion among close friends, indirectly (~280,000 votes), for roughly 340,000 additional votes; the social-transmission effect exceeded the direct message effect.
