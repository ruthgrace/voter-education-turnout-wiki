> Converse, Philip E. 1990. "Popular Representation and the Distribution of Information." In Information and Democratic Processes, ed. John A. Ferejohn and James H. Kuklinski. Urbana: University of Illinois Press, pp. 369–388.

**Source:** [View publication ↗](https://www.google.com/books/edition/Information_and_Democratic_Processes/hd6-AAAAIAAJ) · Published 1990

Examines how political information is distributed across the electorate and coined the "miracle of aggregation" — the idea that random errors by low-information citizens cancel out so that aggregate opinion reflects the views of the informed. A canonical source for the aggregation defense of mass democratic competence later contested by Bartels and Achen & Bartels.
