> Gronke, Paul, Eva Galanes-Rosenbaum, Peter A. Miller, and Daniel Toffey. 2008. "Convenience Voting." Annual Review of Political Science 11: 437–455.

**Source:** [View publication ↗](https://doi.org/10.1146/annurev.polisci.11.053006.190912) · Published 2008

A synthesizing review of "convenience voting" modes (early in-person, vote-by-mail, absentee, electronic), which by then accounted for over 30% of ballots. Concludes that convenience reforms mostly retain existing voters rather than mobilize new ones, and that all-mail elections show the most consistent (if modest) turnout gains.
