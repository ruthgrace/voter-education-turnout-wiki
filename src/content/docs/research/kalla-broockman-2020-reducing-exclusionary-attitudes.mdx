> Kalla, Joshua L., and David E. Broockman. 2020. "Reducing Exclusionary Attitudes Through Interpersonal Conversation: Evidence from Three Field Experiments." American Political Science Review 114(2): 410–425.

**Source:** [View publication ↗](https://doi.org/10.1017/S0003055419000923) · Published 2020

Three pre-registered field experiments (230 canvassers, 6,869 voters, 7 US locations) tested deep canvassing on attitudes toward unauthorized immigrants and transgender people. Argument-only conversations had no effect, but conversations adding non-judgmental exchange of personal narratives durably reduced exclusionary attitudes for at least four months, supporting the scalability of the approach.
