> Miller, Peter, and Ruth Dassonneville. 2016. "High Turnout in the Low Countries: Partisan Effects of the Abolition of Compulsory Voting in the Netherlands." Electoral Studies 44: 132–143.

**Source:** [View publication ↗](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.electstud.2016.08.009) · Published 2016

Uses the 1970 abolition of compulsory voting in the Netherlands as a quasi-experiment to test whether high turnout benefits the left. Finds that compulsory voting (higher turnout) boosted Dutch social-democratic vote share while suppressing minor and extreme parties — qualified support for the conventional partisan-turnout hypothesis.
