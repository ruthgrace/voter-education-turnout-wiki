> Nickerson, David W. 2015. "Do Voter Registration Drives Increase Participation? For Whom and When?" The Journal of Politics 77(1): 88–101.

**Source:** [View publication ↗](https://doi.org/10.1086/678391) · Published 2015

Using field experiments across six cities in which 620 streets were randomly assigned to receive door-to-door registration canvassing, isolates the causal effect of registration drives free of self-selection. Finds drives add genuinely new voters to the electorate (rather than merely processing people who would have registered anyway), with effects concentrated among lower-propensity individuals.
