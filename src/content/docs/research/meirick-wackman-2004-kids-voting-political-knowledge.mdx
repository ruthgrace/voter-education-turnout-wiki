> Meirick, Patrick C., and Daniel B. Wackman. 2004. "Kids Voting and Political Knowledge: Narrowing Gaps, Informing Votes." Social Science Quarterly 85(5): 1161–1177.

**Source:** [View publication ↗](https://doi.org/10.1111/j.0038-4941.2004.00269.x) · Published 2004

Evaluates the Kids Voting USA program (classroom curriculum culminating in a student mock election on Election Day) and finds it raises students' political knowledge, partly by stimulating news attention and family political discussion. The program narrows knowledge gaps and supports more informed voting.
