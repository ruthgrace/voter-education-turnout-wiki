> Kuklinski, James H., Paul J. Quirk, Jennifer Jerit, David Schwieder, and Robert F. Rich. 2000. "Misinformation and the Currency of Democratic Citizenship." The Journal of Politics 62(3): 790–816.

**Source:** [View publication ↗](https://doi.org/10.1111/0022-3816.00033) · Published 2000

Using survey experiments on welfare, the authors distinguish being uninformed from being confidently misinformed and show that many citizens hold factually wrong beliefs while being highly certain of them. They demonstrate that misinformation, not mere ignorance, distorts collective policy preferences away from what people would prefer if correctly informed, and that simply providing facts often does not dislodge the misperception.
