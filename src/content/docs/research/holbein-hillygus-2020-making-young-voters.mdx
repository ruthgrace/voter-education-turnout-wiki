> Holbein, John B., and D. Sunshine Hillygus. 2020. Making Young Voters: Converting Civic Attitudes into Civic Action. New York: Cambridge University Press.

**Source:** [View publication ↗](https://doi.org/10.1017/9781108770446) · Published 2020

Argues the youth turnout gap (roughly 28 points below over-60s in presidential years) stems not from apathy but from a follow-through gap: young people intend to vote but lack the noncognitive skills (self-regulation, persistence) to overcome registration and logistical hurdles. Recommends building noncognitive skills through civic education plus lowering barriers via preregistration and same-day registration.
