> Fowler, Anthony. 2013. "Electoral and Policy Consequences of Voter Turnout: Evidence from Compulsory Voting in Australia." Quarterly Journal of Political Science 8(2): 159–182.

**Source:** [View publication ↗](https://doi.org/10.1561/100.00012055) · Published 2013

Uses the staggered adoption of compulsory voting across Australian states as a natural experiment, showing it raised turnout by ~24 points and increased Labor (left) vote and seat shares by 7–10 points. Australia's adoption also significantly raised pension spending — direct evidence that higher turnout shifts both election and policy outcomes.
