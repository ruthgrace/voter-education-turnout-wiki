> Brams, Steven J., and Peter C. Fishburn. 2007. Approval Voting, 2nd ed. New York: Springer. (1st ed. 1983.)

**Source:** [View publication ↗](https://link.springer.com/book/10.1007/978-0-387-49896-6) · Published 2007

The foundational theoretical treatment of approval voting, where voters may approve as many candidates as they wish and the most-approved candidate wins. Argues on axiomatic and strategic grounds that approval voting is more likely than plurality or runoff to elect broadly acceptable (Condorcet) winners, reduces wasted/strategic voting, and raises the winner's legitimacy. (Scholarly monograph.)
