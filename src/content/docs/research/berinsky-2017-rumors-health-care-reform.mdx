> Berinsky, Adam J. 2017. "Rumors and Health Care Reform: Experiments in Political Misinformation." British Journal of Political Science 47(2): 241–262.

**Source:** [View publication ↗](https://doi.org/10.1017/S0007123415000186) · Published 2017

Through experiments on rumors about the 2010 health care reform ("death panels"), Berinsky shows that rumors gain power through familiarity, so directly repeating a rumor to refute it can increase belief in it via fluency. Corrections from surprising or unlikely sources (e.g., co-partisan elites contradicting expectations) were more effective at getting citizens to reject rumors than ordinary rebuttals.
