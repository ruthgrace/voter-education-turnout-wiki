> Page, Benjamin I., and Robert Y. Shapiro. 1992. The Rational Public: Fifty Years of Trends in Americans' Policy Preferences. Chicago: University of Chicago Press.

**Source:** [View publication ↗](https://press.uchicago.edu/ucp/books/book/chicago/R/bo3762628.html) · Published 1992

Analyzes fifty years of U.S. public-opinion survey trends and argues that, although individuals are poorly informed, collective public opinion is stable, coherent, and responds sensibly to new events and information. A key statement of the "miracle of aggregation" — that individual errors cancel out, yielding a rational aggregate public.
