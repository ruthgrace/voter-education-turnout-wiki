> Hersh, Eitan D. 2015. Hacking the Electorate: How Campaigns Perceive Voters. New York: Cambridge University Press.

**Source:** [View publication ↗](https://doi.org/10.1017/CBO9781316212783) · Published 2015

Hersh argues that campaigns' microtargeting is constrained by the public records (voter files, administrative data) available in each state, so campaigns act on "perceived voters" — data avatars rather than real people. Because states collect different records, campaign targeting and mobilization coalitions vary across the country, and microtargeting is far cruder than commonly assumed.
