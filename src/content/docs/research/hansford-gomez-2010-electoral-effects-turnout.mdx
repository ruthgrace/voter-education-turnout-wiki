> Hansford, Thomas G., and Brad T. Gomez. 2010. "Estimating the Electoral Effects of Voter Turnout." American Political Science Review 104(2): 268–288.

**Source:** [View publication ↗](https://doi.org/10.1017/S0003055410000109) · Published 2010

Uses Election Day rainfall as an instrument for turnout across U.S. counties (1948–2000 presidential elections) to break the endogeneity between turnout and vote choice. Finds higher turnout modestly aids Democrats, hurts incumbents, and makes outcomes more volatile/less predictable.
