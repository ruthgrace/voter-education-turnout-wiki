> Campbell, David E. 2008. "Voice in the Classroom: How an Open Classroom Climate Fosters Political Engagement Among Adolescents." Political Behavior 30(4): 437–454.

**Source:** [View publication ↗](https://doi.org/10.1007/s11109-008-9063-z) · Published 2008

Using the 1999 IEA CIVED survey of 9th-graders, Campbell shows that an open classroom climate (students feeling free to discuss and disagree about political/social issues) raises adolescents' civic knowledge, appreciation of political conflict, and intention to vote as informed citizens. The effect partially compensates for low socioeconomic status, narrowing participation gaps.
