> Lau, Richard R., and David P. Redlawsk. 2001. "Advantages and Disadvantages of Cognitive Heuristics in Political Decision Making." American Journal of Political Science 45(4): 951–971.

**Source:** [View publication ↗](https://www.jstor.org/stable/2669334) · Published 2001

Uses a dynamic process-tracing experiment in a mock presidential campaign to test whether five common heuristics (party, ideology, endorsements, polls, candidate appearance) actually improve voter decisions. Finds heuristics help political experts vote correctly but can hurt low-knowledge voters, challenging the assumption that cues let uninformed voters behave as if informed.
