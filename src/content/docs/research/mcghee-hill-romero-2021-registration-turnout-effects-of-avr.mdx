> McGhee, Eric, Charlotte Hill, and Mindy Romero. 2021. "The Registration and Turnout Effects of Automatic Voter Registration." SSRN Working Paper No. 3933442 (USC CCEP / PPIC).

**Source:** [View publication ↗](https://papers.ssrn.com/sol3/papers.cfm?abstract_id=3933442) · Published 2021

Using a difference-in-differences design across states, this systematic study of automatic voter registration (AVR) design finds effects depend heavily on implementation. Back-end default AVR raised registration by about 8.1% and eligible turnout by about 3.3%; front-end default raised registration ~2.9% and turnout ~1.1%. AVR adds many registrants who are individually less likely to vote, yet enough do that overall eligible turnout still rises. (Working paper.)
