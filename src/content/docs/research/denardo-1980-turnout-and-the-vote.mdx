> DeNardo, James. 1980. "Turnout and the Vote: The Joke's on the Democrats?" American Political Science Review 74(2): 406–420.

**Source:** [View publication ↗](https://doi.org/10.2307/1960636) · Published 1980

A foundational theoretical and empirical challenge to the conventional wisdom that high turnout helps Democrats. DeNardo argues that surge voters are more likely to defect from their nominal party ties, so higher turnout brings in "peripheral" voters whose defection can benefit the minority party, meaning increased turnout does not reliably advantage Democrats.
