> Farrell, David M., and Ian McAllister. 2006. "Voter Satisfaction and Electoral Systems: Does Preferential Voting in Candidate-Centred Systems Make a Difference?" European Journal of Political Research 45(5): 723–749.

**Source:** [View publication ↗](https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1475-6765.2006.00633.x) · Published 2006

Moves beyond the PR/non-PR dichotomy to ballot structure — whether voters rank candidates (preferential/STV, AV) — using CSES data. Finds preferential, candidate-centered systems are associated with higher voter satisfaction with democracy, suggesting the added ballot complexity is offset by voters' sense of fairness and expressed choice rather than harming the voter experience.
