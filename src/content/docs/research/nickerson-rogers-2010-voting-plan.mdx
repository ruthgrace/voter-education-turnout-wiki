> Nickerson, David W., and Todd Rogers. 2010. "Do You Have a Voting Plan? Implementation Intentions, Voter Turnout, and Organic Plan Making." Psychological Science 21(2): 194–199.

**Source:** [View publication ↗](https://doi.org/10.1177/0956797609359326) · Published 2010

A large randomized phone-call experiment (~287,000 contacts) in the 2008 presidential primary tested scripts that helped voters form a concrete "voting plan" (when, from where, what they'll be doing before). Plan-making calls increased turnout more than standard GOTV calls, especially in single-eligible-voter households, showing script content shapes call effectiveness.
