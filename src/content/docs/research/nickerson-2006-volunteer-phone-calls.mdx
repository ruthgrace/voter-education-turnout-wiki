> Nickerson, David W. 2006. "Volunteer Phone Calls Can Increase Turnout: Evidence from Eight Field Experiments." American Politics Research 34(3): 271–292.

**Source:** [View publication ↗](https://doi.org/10.1177/1532673X05275923) · Published 2006

Pooled eight randomized nonpartisan volunteer phone-bank experiments across six cities in 2000–2001. Volunteer calls raised turnout by about 3.8 percentage points and were cost-competitive with canvassing (roughly one additional vote per $26), challenging the earlier finding that phone calls do not work.
