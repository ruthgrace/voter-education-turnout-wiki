> Nichols, Stephen M., and Gregory A. Strizek. 1995. "Electronic Voting Machines and Ballot Roll-Off." American Politics Quarterly 23(3): 300–318.

**Source:** [View publication ↗](https://doi.org/10.1177/1532673X9502300303) · Published 1995

County-level analysis of how voting technology affects roll-off. Finds electronic voting machines — especially those that flag or alert voters to skipped contests — sharply reduce roll-off in low-visibility down-ballot races, net of socioeconomic and fatigue controls, showing that point-of-decision prompting (not just voter knowledge) drives completion.
