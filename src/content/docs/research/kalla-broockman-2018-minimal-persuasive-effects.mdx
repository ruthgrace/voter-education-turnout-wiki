> Kalla, Joshua L., and David E. Broockman. 2018. "The Minimal Persuasive Effects of Campaign Contact in General Elections: Evidence from 49 Field Experiments." American Political Science Review 112(1): 148–166.

**Source:** [View publication ↗](https://doi.org/10.1017/S0003055417000363) · Published 2018

A meta-analysis of 40 field experiments plus nine new original experiments finds the best estimate of the average effect of campaign contact and advertising on candidate choice in general elections is approximately zero. Persuasive effects can appear earlier in campaigns, in primaries/ballot measures, or when messaging engages unfamiliar issues, but general-election persuasion via contact and ads is minimal.
