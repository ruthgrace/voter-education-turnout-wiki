> Burden, Barry C., David T. Canon, Kenneth R. Mayer, and Donald P. Moynihan. 2014. "Election Laws, Mobilization, and Turnout: The Unanticipated Consequences of Election Reform." American Journal of Political Science 58(1): 95–109.

**Source:** [View publication ↗](https://doi.org/10.1111/ajps.12063) · Published 2014

Using aggregate and individual-level analyses of the 2004 and 2008 elections, shows Election Day (same-day) registration consistently raises turnout, whereas early voting by itself is associated with lower turnout because it dissipates the mobilizing energy of Election Day. Combining early voting with same-day registration offsets the negative effect.
