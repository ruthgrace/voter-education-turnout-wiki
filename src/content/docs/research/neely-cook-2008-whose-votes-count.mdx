> Neely, Francis, and Corey Cook. 2008. "Whose Votes Count? Undervotes, Overvotes, and Ranking in San Francisco's Instant-Runoff Elections." American Politics Research 36(4): 530–554.

**Source:** [View publication ↗](https://doi.org/10.1177/1532673X08318110) · Published 2008

Uses individual ballot records plus precinct-level census data from the first three years of San Francisco IRV to model undervoting, overvoting, and ballot ranking. Finds the switch to IRV raised overvote rates and lowered undervote rates, with both patterned by demographics and electoral context; ranking behavior was driven more by context and prior IRV exposure than by demographics.
