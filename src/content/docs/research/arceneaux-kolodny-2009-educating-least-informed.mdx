> Arceneaux, Kevin, and Robin Kolodny. 2009. "Educating the Least Informed: Group Endorsements in a Grassroots Campaign." American Journal of Political Science 53(4): 755–770.

**Source:** [View publication ↗](https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1540-5907.2009.00399.x) · Published 2009

A field experiment during two competitive 2006 Pennsylvania statehouse races, in which a liberal interest group canvassed both core supporters and likely-likeminded Republicans, tests whether group endorsements help uninformed voters vote consistent with their predispositions. The endorsement cue moved the least politically aware, ideologically-aligned voters toward the endorsed candidate — but cue effects depended on voters recognizing the group's relevance.
