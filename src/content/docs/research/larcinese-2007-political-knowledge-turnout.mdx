> Larcinese, Valentino. 2007. "Does Political Knowledge Increase Turnout? Evidence from the 1997 British General Election." Public Choice 131(3–4): 387–411.

**Source:** [View publication ↗](https://doi.org/10.1007/s11127-006-9122-0) · Published 2007

Uses 1997 British Election Study data and instrumental-variable strategies to estimate the effect of political knowledge on turnout. Finds information is among the strongest predictors of participation — raising knowledge one standard deviation increases the probability of voting by roughly 6 percentage points — while addressing endogeneity between knowledge and voting.
