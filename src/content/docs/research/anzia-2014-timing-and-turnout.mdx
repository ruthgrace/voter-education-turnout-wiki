> Anzia, Sarah F. 2014. Timing and Turnout: How Off-Cycle Elections Favor Organized Groups. Chicago: University of Chicago Press.

**Source:** [View publication ↗](https://press.uchicago.edu/ucp/books/book/chicago/T/bo16956602.html) · Published 2014

Book-length treatment showing that most U.S. officials are elected in low-turnout off-cycle contests, which amplifies the influence of teachers' unions and municipal employee groups. Includes analysis of California city elections, arguing that election-timing choices are themselves politically contested because on-cycle consolidation dilutes organized-group power.
