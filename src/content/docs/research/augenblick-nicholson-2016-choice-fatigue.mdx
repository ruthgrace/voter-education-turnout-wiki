> Augenblick, Ned, and Scott Nicholson. 2016. "Ballot Position, Choice Fatigue, and Voter Behaviour." The Review of Economic Studies 83(2): 460–480.

**Source:** [View publication ↗](https://doi.org/10.1093/restud/rdv047) · Published 2016

Natural experiment: the same contest appears at different ballot positions across San Diego County precincts because of variation in the number of local items above it. Facing more prior decisions significantly raises abstention and reliance on shortcuts (status-quo/first-listed choice). The authors estimate that eliminating choice fatigue would cut abstentions by ~8% and would have flipped ~6% of propositions from fail to pass.
