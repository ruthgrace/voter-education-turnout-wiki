> Gerber, Alan S., and Donald P. Green. 2000. "The Effects of Canvassing, Telephone Calls, and Direct Mail on Voter Turnout: A Field Experiment." American Political Science Review 94(3): 653–663.

**Source:** [View publication ↗](https://doi.org/10.2307/2585837) · Published 2000

A randomized nonpartisan GOTV field experiment on roughly 30,000 registered voters in New Haven, CT before the 1998 election, comparing door-to-door canvassing, direct mail, and commercial phone calls. Face-to-face canvassing raised turnout by about 8–10 points, direct mail had small effects (about 0.5 point per mailer), and professional phone calls had no detectable effect. The study launched the modern GOTV field-experiment paradigm.
