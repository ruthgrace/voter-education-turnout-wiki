> Lau, Richard R., David J. Andersen, and David P. Redlawsk. 2008. "An Exploration of Correct Voting in Recent U.S. Presidential Elections." American Journal of Political Science 52(2): 395–411.

**Source:** [View publication ↗](https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1540-5907.2008.00319.x) · Published 2008

Applies the fully-informed-preferences method to nine U.S. presidential elections (1972–2004) using ANES data. Finds an average of just over three-quarters of voters voted correctly, and identifies campaign- and individual-level factors (e.g., information availability, campaign clarity) that raise or lower correct-voting rates.
