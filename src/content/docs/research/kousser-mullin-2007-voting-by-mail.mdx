> Kousser, Thad, and Megan Mullin. 2007. "Does Voting by Mail Increase Participation? Using Matching to Analyze a Natural Experiment." Political Analysis 15(4): 428–445.

**Source:** [View publication ↗](https://doi.org/10.1093/pan/mpm014) · Published 2007

Uses a California natural experiment in which precincts were assigned to all-mail voting in a near-random process, then matches mail and polling-place precincts to isolate the effect. Finds voting by mail did not raise (and may slightly depress) turnout in high-salience general elections, but modestly increased participation in low-turnout special elections — challenging the assumption that convenience reforms uniformly boost turnout.
