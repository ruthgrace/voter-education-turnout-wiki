> Neundorf, Anja, Richard G. Niemi, and Kaat Smets. 2016. "The Compensation Effect of Civic Education on Political Engagement: How Civics Classes Make Up for Missing Parental Socialization." Political Behavior 38(4): 921–949.

**Source:** [View publication ↗](https://doi.org/10.1007/s11109-016-9341-0) · Published 2016

Using the U.S. Youth-Parent Socialization Panel (1965–1997) and the Belgian Political Panel (2006–2011), finds that civics coursework compensates for weak parental political socialization, raising engagement most for youth from low-politicization homes. The equalizing effect holds across two countries and eras.
