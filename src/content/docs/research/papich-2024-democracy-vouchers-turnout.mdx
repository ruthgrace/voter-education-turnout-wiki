> Papich, Sarah. 2024. "Do Democracy Vouchers Help Democracy?" Contemporary Economic Policy 42(1): 4–24.

**Source:** [View publication ↗](https://doi.org/10.1111/coep.12625) · Published 2024

Billed as the first study of public campaign financing's effect on voter participation (not just candidate finances), it uses Seattle's staggered exposure to vouchers to estimate turnout effects. The program is estimated to increase voter turnout by about 4.9 percentage points, and voucher users participated in more subsequent elections than non-users.
