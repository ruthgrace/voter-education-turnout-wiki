> Delli Carpini, Michael X., and Scott Keeter. 1996. What Americans Know about Politics and Why It Matters. New Haven, CT: Yale University Press.

**Source:** [View publication ↗](https://www.jstor.org/stable/j.ctt1cc2kv1) · Published 1996

The most comprehensive analysis of the level and distribution of political knowledge in the U.S. public, drawing on decades of survey data. Finds knowledge is low on average and highly unequally distributed (by race, gender, age, and income), and that better-informed citizens participate more, better identify their own interests, and connect preferences to political action.
