> Lijphart, Arend. 1997. "Unequal Participation: Democracy's Unresolved Dilemma. Presidential Address, American Political Science Association, 1996." American Political Science Review 91(1): 1–14.

**Source:** [View publication ↗](https://doi.org/10.2307/2952255) · Published 1997

Landmark presidential address arguing that low turnout is systematically class-biased — less well-off citizens vote less — so unequal turnout produces unequal political influence, a fundamental democratic problem. Lijphart proposes institutional fixes to maximize turnout, most powerfully compulsory voting, and frames the "who votes matters" agenda that defines this literature.
