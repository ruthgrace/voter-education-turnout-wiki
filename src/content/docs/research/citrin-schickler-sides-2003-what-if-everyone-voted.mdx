> Citrin, Jack, Eric Schickler, and John Sides. 2003. "What If Everyone Voted? Simulating the Impact of Increased Turnout in Senate Elections." American Journal of Political Science 47(1): 75–90.

**Source:** [View publication ↗](https://doi.org/10.1111/1540-5907.00006) · Published 2003

Using state exit-poll and Census data, estimates nonvoters' partisan preferences and simulates Senate election outcomes under universal turnout. Although nonvoters lean somewhat more Democratic, so few races are close that virtually no outcomes would flip, undercutting the claim that full turnout would systematically help Democrats.
