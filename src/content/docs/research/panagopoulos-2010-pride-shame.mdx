> Panagopoulos, Costas. 2010. "Affect, Social Pressure and Prosocial Motivation: Field Experimental Evidence of the Mobilizing Effects of Pride, Shame and Publicizing Voting Behavior." Political Behavior 32(3): 369–386.

**Source:** [View publication ↗](https://doi.org/10.1007/s11109-010-9114-0) · Published 2010

Three field experiments in the November 2007 elections tested mailings threatening to publicize voters' turnout — honoring voters ("pride") or shaming abstainers ("shame") by listing names in the local newspaper. Both pride and shame appeals significantly increased turnout, extending the social-pressure findings to affect-based prosocial motivation.
