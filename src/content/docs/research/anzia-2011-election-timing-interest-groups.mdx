> Anzia, Sarah F. 2011. "Election Timing and the Electoral Influence of Interest Groups." The Journal of Politics 73(2): 412–427.

**Source:** [View publication ↗](https://doi.org/10.1017/S0022381611000028) · Published 2011

Argues off-cycle elections depress turnout, which magnifies the leverage of organized groups whose members vote regardless of timing. Using U.S. school-district data, finds districts with off-cycle elections pay experienced teachers over 3% more than on-cycle districts — evidence that timing shifts policy toward mobilized interests.
