> Malhotra, Neil, Melissa R. Michelson, Todd Rogers, and Ali Adam Valenzuela. 2011. "Text Messages as Mobilization Tools: Implications for Theories of Political Participation." American Politics Research 39(4): 664–681.

**Source:** [View publication ↗](https://doi.org/10.1177/1532673X11398949) · Published 2011

A field experiment testing "cold" text messages (with no prior personal contact or opt-in interaction). Even auxiliary-interaction-free texts significantly boosted turnout, challenging the claim that social connectedness is necessary for mobilization and reinforcing a salience/reminder mechanism.
