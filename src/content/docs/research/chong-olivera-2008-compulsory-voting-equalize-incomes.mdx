> Chong, Alberto, and Mauricio Olivera. 2008. "Does Compulsory Voting Help Equalize Incomes?" Economics & Politics 20(3): 391–415.

**Source:** [View publication ↗](https://doi.org/10.1111/j.1468-0343.2008.00336.x) · Published 2008

Cross-country analysis of 91 countries (1960–2000) testing Lijphart's proposition that mandatory turnout reduces inequality. Finds that where compulsory voting is strongly enforced, income distribution improves (lower Gini, gains for bottom quintiles) — empirical support that turnout equality can feed into economic policy outcomes.
