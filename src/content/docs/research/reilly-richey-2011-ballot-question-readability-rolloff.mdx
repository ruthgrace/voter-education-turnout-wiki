> Reilly, Shauna, and Sean Richey. 2011. "Ballot Question Readability and Roll-Off: The Impact of Language Complexity." Political Research Quarterly 64(1): 59–67.

**Source:** [View publication ↗](https://doi.org/10.1177/1065912909349629) · Published 2011

Computes Flesch-Kincaid grade-level readability scores for 1,211 statewide ballot measures (1997–2007) and models roll-off with hierarchical regression. Finds that more linguistically complex (harder-to-read) ballot questions produce significantly higher roll-off, net of confounders — so the comprehensibility of the ballot text itself shapes whether voters complete measures.
