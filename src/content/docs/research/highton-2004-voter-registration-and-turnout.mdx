> Highton, Benjamin. 2004. "Voter Registration and Turnout in the United States." Perspectives on Politics 2(3): 507–515.

**Source:** [View publication ↗](https://doi.org/10.1017/S1537592704040307) · Published 2004

A review essay tracing how U.S. registration laws (from the late 19th century through the 2000s) shape turnout levels, which groups are most affected, and the partisan implications. Concludes registration barriers depress turnout, especially among lower-status citizens, but that easing them yields smaller partisan effects than commonly assumed.
