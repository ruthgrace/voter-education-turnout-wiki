> Berry, Christopher R., and Jacob E. Gersen. 2010. "The Timing of Elections." University of Chicago Law Review 77(1): 37–64. (Law review — not peer-reviewed.)

**Source:** [View publication ↗](https://chicagounbound.uchicago.edu/uclrev/vol77/iss1/3/) · Published 2010

Theorizes and documents "selective participation": when local elections are decoupled from federal/state contests, the cost of voting rises so that only high-stakes voters turn out. Argues the sheer number and off-cycle scheduling of U.S. elections systematically shapes who participates and, in turn, policy.
