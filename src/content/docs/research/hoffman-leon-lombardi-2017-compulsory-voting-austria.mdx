> Hoffman, Mitchell, Gianmarco León, and María Lombardi. 2017. "Compulsory Voting, Turnout, and Government Spending: Evidence from Austria." Journal of Public Economics 145: 103–115.

**Source:** [View publication ↗](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jpubeco.2016.10.002) · Published 2017

Exploits within-Austria variation in compulsory voting laws over time, finding compulsion raised turnout by roughly 10 points. Despite the turnout increase, they find little evidence of effects on the composition or size of government spending, a cautionary counterpoint to claims that turnout reliably reshapes policy.
