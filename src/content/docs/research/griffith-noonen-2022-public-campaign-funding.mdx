> Griffith, Alan, and Thomas Noonen. 2022. "The Effects of Public Campaign Funding: Evidence from Seattle's Democracy Voucher Program." Journal of Public Economics 211: 104676.

**Source:** [View publication ↗](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.jpubeco.2022.104676) · Published 2022

A difference-in-differences design compares Seattle to large Washington and California cities before and after the 2017 voucher rollout. The program raised total contributions by ~53% and the number of unique donors by ~350% (concentrated in sub-$200 gifts), increased the number of candidates by ~86%, and weakened incumbent electoral performance.
