> Lau, Richard R., and David P. Redlawsk. 1997. "Voting Correctly." American Political Science Review 91(3): 585–598.

**Source:** [View publication ↗](https://doi.org/10.2307/2952076) · Published 1997

Introduces the concept of "correct voting" — voting the way one would have with full information — and offers two methods for measuring it (a normative fully-informed-preferences benchmark and a process-tracing experimental benchmark). Finds roughly 75% of voters vote correctly, an optimistic counterpoint to the pessimistic knowledge literature.
