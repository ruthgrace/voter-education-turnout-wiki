> Yorgason, Chenoa. 2024. "Campaign Finance Vouchers Do Not Expand the Diversity of Donors: Evidence from Seattle." American Political Science Review 119(1): 508–516.

**Source:** [View publication ↗](https://doi.org/10.1017/S0003055424000170) · Published 2024

Using panel data linking voter demographics to contributions across nine electoral cycles (2005–2021), finds the voters most mobilized by vouchers already belong to groups overrepresented in the donor pool. Participation by young, nonwhite, and Republican residents rose at significantly lower rates than dominant groups, so vouchers "do not systematically improve the diversity of the donor pool" — a direct counterpoint to McCabe & Heerwig.
