> Thompson, Daniel M., Jennifer A. Wu, Jesse Yoder, and Andrew B. Hall. 2020. "Universal Vote-by-Mail Has No Impact on Partisan Turnout or Vote Share." Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences 117(25): 14052–14056.

**Source:** [View publication ↗](https://doi.org/10.1073/pnas.2007249117) · Published 2020

Using a difference-in-differences design on county-level data from states that rolled out universal vote-by-mail (every voter automatically mailed a ballot), the authors estimate its causal effects. They find UVBM modestly raises overall turnout (roughly 2 percentage points) but has no detectable effect on either party's share of turnout or vote share, contradicting claims that VBM advantages Democrats.
