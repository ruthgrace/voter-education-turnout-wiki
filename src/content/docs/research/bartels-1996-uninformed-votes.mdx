> Bartels, Larry M. 1996. "Uninformed Votes: Information Effects in Presidential Elections." American Journal of Political Science 40(1): 194–230.

**Source:** [View publication ↗](https://www.jstor.org/stable/2111700) · Published 1996

Uses 1972–1992 ANES data to simulate how vote choices would differ if voters were "fully informed," matching informed and uninformed voters with similar demographics. Finds substantial information effects: cues/shortcuts do not fully compensate for low information, and individual deviations do not cancel out in the aggregate, so incumbents and Democrats were systematically advantaged by low information.
