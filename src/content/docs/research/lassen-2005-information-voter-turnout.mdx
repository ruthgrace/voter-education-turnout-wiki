> Lassen, David Dreyer. 2005. "The Effect of Information on Voter Turnout: Evidence from a Natural Experiment." American Journal of Political Science 49(1): 103–118.

**Source:** [View publication ↗](https://doi.org/10.1111/j.0092-5853.2005.00113.x) · Published 2005

Exploits a Copenhagen decentralization pilot in which four representative districts were assigned local administration while others were not, then compares turnout in a citywide referendum. Finds that being informed (through direct experience with the treated policy) causally increased turnout, giving rare quasi-experimental evidence that information boosts participation.
