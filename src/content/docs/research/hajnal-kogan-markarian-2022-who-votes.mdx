> Hajnal, Zoltan L., Vladimir Kogan, and G. Agustin Markarian. 2022. "Who Votes: City Election Timing and Voter Composition." American Political Science Review 116(1): 374–383.

**Source:** [View publication ↗](https://doi.org/10.1017/S0003055421000915) · Published 2022

Uses California cities forced to move local elections on-cycle to estimate effects on the composition of the electorate. On-cycle timing roughly doubles turnout and makes the electorate substantially more representative by race, age, and partisanship; the share of local voters over age 55 fell from about half to 28% after consolidation.
