> Baujard, Antoinette, Frédéric Gavrel, Herrade Igersheim, Jean-François Laslier, and Isabelle Lebon. 2014. "Who's Favored by Evaluative Voting? An Experiment Conducted During the 2012 French Presidential Election." Electoral Studies 34: 131–145.

**Source:** [View publication ↗](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.electstud.2013.11.003) · Published 2014

An in-situ experiment with ~2,340 participants across three French towns during the 2012 first round, comparing approval and grade-based (evaluative) voting against the official two-round system. Finds the two-round/plurality system favors "exclusive" candidates who arouse strong feelings, whereas evaluative and approval rules favor "inclusive" candidates with broad, moderate support — shifting outcomes toward more consensual winners.
