> Martin, Paul S., and Michele P. Claibourn. 2013. "Citizen Participation and Congressional Responsiveness: New Evidence that Participation Matters." Legislative Studies Quarterly 38(1): 5–34.

**Source:** [View publication ↗](https://doi.org/10.1111/lsq.12003) · Published 2013

Argues legislators read district turnout as a signal of scrutiny and sanction risk, so higher-participation districts receive greater policy responsiveness. Using nearly four decades of U.S. House data, finds districts with higher voting rates are rewarded with stronger congruence between representative behavior and constituent preferences.
