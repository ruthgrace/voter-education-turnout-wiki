> Leighley, Jan E., and Jonathan Nagler. 2013. Who Votes Now? Demographics, Issues, Inequality, and Turnout in the United States. Princeton, NJ: Princeton University Press.

**Source:** [View publication ↗](https://press.princeton.edu/books/paperback/9780691159355/who-votes-now) · Published 2013

Drawing on Census CPS and ANES data from 1972–2008, compares the demographic profiles and policy preferences of voters versus nonvoters. Documents a persistent class bias (the rich vote more than the poor, and voters are more economically conservative than nonvoters), a closed black-white gap, and shows that reforms like Election Day registration and easier absentee/early voting boost turnout.
