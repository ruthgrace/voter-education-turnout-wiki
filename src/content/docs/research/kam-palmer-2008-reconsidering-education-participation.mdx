> Kam, Cindy D., and Carl L. Palmer. 2008. "Reconsidering the Effects of Education on Political Participation." The Journal of Politics 70(3): 612–631.

**Source:** [View publication ↗](https://doi.org/10.1017/S0022381608080651) · Published 2008

Using propensity-score matching on the 1965–1997 Youth-Parent Socialization Panel, argues that once preadult experiences and family background are accounted for, higher education has little independent causal effect on participation — education is largely a proxy for earlier socialization. The influential skeptical counterpoint to the education-causes-participation consensus. (Later work, e.g. Henderson & Chatfield, contests these results.)
